The Soaring Pteranodon

The Pteranodon was a large flying reptile that lived during the late Cretaceous period, about 70 to 100 million years ago. Although not technically a dinosaur, it was one of the most famous pterosaurs, known for its impressive wingspan and long, toothless beak.

Some Facts About Pteranodon:

  • It had a wingspan of up to 23 feet (7 meters).
  • Its name means “winged and toothless.”
  • It likely fed on fish, catching them while gliding over the ocean.
  • Male Pteranodons had large head crests, possibly used for display or balance in flight.

Scientists believe that Pteranodon spent much of its life soaring over coastal waters, using ocean breezes to glide effortlessly for long distances. Its aerodynamic body and strong wings made it one of the most skilled flyers of the prehistoric skies.
