The Mighty Tyrannosaurus Rex

The Tyrannosaurus Rex, often called T-Rex, was one of the largest meat-eating dinosaurs that ever lived. It existed during the late Cretaceous period, about 68 to 66 million years ago.

Some Facts About T-Rex:

  • It could grow up to 40 feet long and 12 feet tall at the hips.
  • Its bite was strong enough to crush bones easily.
  • T-Rex had small arms but very powerful legs.
  • It was a top predator of its time.

Scientists believe that T-Rex had a very good sense of smell and sharp vision, making it an excellent hunter. Even though it was feared, it played a very important role in keeping the balance of its ecosystem.
