The Crested Parasaurolophus

The Parasaurolophus was a plant-eating dinosaur that lived during the late Cretaceous period, around 76 to 73 million years ago. It was famous for its long, backward-curving crest on its head, which made it one of the most recognizable dinosaurs in history.

Some Facts About Parasaurolophus:

  • It could grow up to 33 feet long and weigh around 2.5 tons.
  • Its hollow crest may have been used to make sounds or attract mates.
  • Parasaurolophus was a herbivore that fed on leaves, pine needles, and fruit.
  • It could walk on both two legs and four legs, depending on its activity.

Scientists believe that the crest of Parasaurolophus acted like a resonating chamber, allowing it to produce deep, trumpet-like sounds for communication. Its gentle nature and unique head shape make it one of the most distinctive and fascinating dinosaurs of the Cretaceous period.
