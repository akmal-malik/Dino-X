The Armored Stegosaurus

The Stegosaurus was a large, plant-eating dinosaur that lived during the late Jurassic period, around 155 to 150 million years ago. It was known for the distinctive double row of large, bony plates along its back and the spiked tail, known as the thagomizer.

Some Facts About Stegosaurus:

  • It could grow up to 30 feet long and weigh around 5 to 7 tons.
  • Its brain was about the size of a walnut — quite small for its body size.
  • The plates on its back may have helped regulate body temperature or been used for display.
  • Its tail had sharp spikes used for defense against predators.

Scientists believe that Stegosaurus, despite its heavy build, was a peaceful herbivore that relied on its tail spikes for defense. Its unique appearance makes it one of the most easily recognizable dinosaurs, symbolizing the diversity of prehistoric life during the Jurassic era.
