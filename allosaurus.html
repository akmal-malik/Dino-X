The Fearsome Allosaurus

The Allosaurus was one of the top predators of the late Jurassic period, living about 155 to 145 million years ago. It was a large carnivorous dinosaur known for its powerful jaws, sharp teeth, and strong hunting abilities.

Some Facts About Allosaurus:

  • It could grow up to 38 feet long and weigh around 2 tons.
  • Allosaurus had sharp, serrated teeth perfect for slicing through flesh.
  • It had three-fingered hands with large claws used to grip prey.
  • Allosaurus likely hunted in packs or groups to take down large herbivores.

Scientists believe that Allosaurus was a fast and intelligent predator, capable of ambushing and overpowering even large dinosaurs like Stegosaurus. Its agility, teamwork, and deadly bite made it one of the most successful hunters of the Jurassic era.
