The Mighty Triceratops

The Triceratops was one of the most recognizable plant-eating dinosaurs that lived during the late Cretaceous period, about 68 million years ago. It was known for its three distinctive facial horns and large bony frill.

Some Facts About Triceratops:

  • It could grow up to 30 feet long and weigh around 12 tons.
  • It had three horns — two above the eyes and one on the nose.
  • Its large frill may have been used for defense or to attract mates.
  • Triceratops was a herbivore, feeding mainly on low-growing plants.

Scientists believe that Triceratops used its horns and frill for both protection against predators like the T-Rex and for displays during social interactions. Despite its fearsome appearance, it was a peaceful herbivore that played a key role in maintaining the balance of its prehistoric environment.
